<p><p>Diagnosis of autoimmune diseases involves various types of tests. Routine laboratory tests analyze blood and urine samples to check for abnormalities in organ function. Inflammatory markers, such as C-reactive protein and erythrocyte sedimentation rate, indicate inflammation in the body. Autoantibodies and immunologic tests detect antibodies that attack healthy tissue. Other tests, such as imaging scans and biopsies, can provide further information on the extent of damage caused by the autoimmune response. These tests help healthcare providers accurately diagnose and treat autoimmune diseases.</p></p>
