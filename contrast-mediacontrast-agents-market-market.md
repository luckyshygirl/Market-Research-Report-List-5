<p><p>Contrast media/contrast agents are substances used in medical imaging to enhance the visibility of internal bodily structures. There are several types of contrast media including Iodinated, Gadolinium, Barium, and Microbubble. Iodinated contrast media are often used in X-ray and CT scans, while Gadolinium is commonly used in MRI scans. Barium contrast media are typically used for imaging the digestive system. Microbubble contrast agents are used primarily in ultrasound imaging to improve images of blood flow. Each type of contrast media serves a specific purpose in enhancing the clarity of medical images.</p></p>
