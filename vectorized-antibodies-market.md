<p><p>Vectorized antibodies are generated using different types of vectors, including Adenoassociated Virus (AAV) Vector, Electroporation, Lipid Nanoparticles (LNPs), and others. AAV vectors are commonly used for gene therapy due to their ability to efficiently deliver genetic material into cells. Electroporation involves applying an electric field to create temporary pores in cell membranes for vector entry. LNPs are lipid-based nanoparticles used for transporting genetic material into cells. Other types of vectors may include retroviruses, lentiviruses, or plasmids.</p></p>
