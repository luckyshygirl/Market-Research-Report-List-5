<p><p>Tooling boards are divided into two main types: polyurethanes and epoxies. Polyurethane tooling boards are known for their low density, ease of machining, and minimal dust creation during cutting. They are commonly used in applications requiring large or intricate molds. Epoxy tooling boards offer high dimensional stability and temperature resistance, making them ideal for applications where precision and durability are key. Both types of tooling boards are widely used in industries such as aerospace, automotive, and marine for prototyping and manufacturing molds.</p></p>
