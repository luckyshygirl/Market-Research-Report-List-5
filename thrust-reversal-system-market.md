<p><p>Thrust Reversal Systems are used in aircraft engines to help slow down the aircraft upon landing by redirecting the engine thrust. There are three main types of Thrust Reversal Systems: Target Type, Clam-shell Type, and Cold stream Type. Target Type systems use movable flaps to redirect the airflow, Clam-shell Type systems have doors that open to deflect the thrust, and Cold stream Type systems redirect the exhaust gases externally. Each type has its own advantages and applications in different aircraft models.</p></p>
