<p><p>Commercial automotive thermal systems are categorized into two types based on their control mechanism: Non-Electronically Controlled Thermal Systems and Electronically Controlled Thermal Systems. Non-Electronically Controlled Thermal Systems rely on mechanical or manual controls to regulate temperature, while Electronically Controlled Thermal Systems use advanced electronics and sensors to automatically adjust the temperature. The latter offer greater precision and efficiency in managing the vehicle's thermal system, leading to improved performance and fuel efficiency. Both types play a crucial role in maintaining optimal temperature levels in commercial vehicles.</p></p>
