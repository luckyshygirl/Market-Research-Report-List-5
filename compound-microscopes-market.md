<p><p>Compound microscopes are available in various types to meet different research and laboratory needs. Vertical microscopes are designed with the objective lens below the stage, making them ideal for viewing samples in containers or large objects. Inverted microscopes, on the other hand, have the objective lens above the stage, making them suitable for observing cells or tissue cultures in dishes or flasks. Both types offer high magnification capabilities and are widely used in fields such as biology, medicine, and material science.</p></p>
