<p><p>Polymerisation inhibitors are chemicals used to prevent the spontaneous polymerisation of monomers during storage or transportation. Different types of inhibitors include Hydroquinone, Quinine, Aromatic Nitro Compounds, Amines, and others. Hydroquinone works by capturing free radicals, Quinine inhibits the formation of peroxides, Aromatic Nitro Compounds act as radical scavengers, Amines react with radicals to form stable radicals, while other inhibitors work through various mechanisms to prevent polymerisation. These inhibitors play a crucial role in ensuring the stability of monomers in the polymerisation process.</p></p>
