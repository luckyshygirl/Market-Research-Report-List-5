<p><p>Hydrogen gas is sold in two main market types: compressed hydrogen gas and liquid hydrogen. </p><p>Compressed hydrogen gas is stored at high pressure in tanks and is commonly used for fuel cell vehicles and industrial applications. It is easier to store and transport compared to liquid hydrogen. </p><p>Liquid hydrogen, on the other hand, is stored at extremely low temperatures (-253°C) and is mainly used in aerospace industry for rocket fuel due to its high energy content. While it requires specialized storage and handling, liquid hydrogen offers higher energy density than compressed gas.</p></p>
