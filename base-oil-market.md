<p><p>Base oil market types are categorized into Group I, II, III, IV, and V based on their refining processes and chemical compositions. </p><p>Group I oils are solvent-refined and have low performance levels. Group II oils are more refined and offer improved performance. Group III oils are highly refined and offer superior quality. Group IV oils are synthetic oils made from polyalphaolefins, while Group V includes all other base oils not included in the previous groups, such as naphthenic oils. Each group offers different levels of purity and performance for various industrial applications.</p></p>
