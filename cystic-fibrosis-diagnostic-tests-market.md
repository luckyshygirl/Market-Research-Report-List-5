<p><p>Genetic tests are used to identify specific genetic mutations associated with cystic fibrosis. Fecal tests can detect high levels of fat in the stool, a common symptom of the disease. The Immunoreactive Trypsinogen (IRT) test measures levels of a protein that may indicate the presence of cystic fibrosis. Sweat chloride tests help diagnose the disease by measuring the amount of chloride in a person's sweat. Pulmonary function tests assess lung function and are crucial for monitoring progression of cystic fibrosis.</p></p>
