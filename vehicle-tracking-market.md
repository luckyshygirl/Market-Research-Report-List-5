<p><p>Vehicle tracking systems can be categorized into two main types: wired tracking and wireless tracking. Wired tracking systems involve the installation of physical wires and cables in the vehicle to transmit data to a central monitoring system. On the other hand, wireless tracking systems use cellular or satellite networks to transmit location and other data wirelessly. Both types of systems have their own advantages and limitations, with wired systems being more reliable but requiring more installation effort, while wireless systems offer more flexibility and easier installation but may have connectivity issues in certain areas.</p></p>
